# Platform profiles (adaptation playbook)

Per-platform guidance for **step 4 (adapt)**. The machine-readable source of
truth is `assets/platforms.json`; this file is the human playbook for rewriting.

## X / Twitter
- **Ideal length:** 80-240 chars (hard cap 280). **Hashtags:** 0-2.
- **Hook:** punchy first line; opinion, number, or hot take.
- **Format:** one idea per post; threads for depth.
- **CTA:** soft (reply, repost). **Media:** image/GIF lifts reach.
- **Best times:** weekdays 9-11am & 6-9pm local. **Cadence:** 1-5/day.

## LinkedIn
- **Ideal length:** 900-1800 chars (cap 3000). **Hashtags:** 3-5.
- **Hook:** first 2 lines must earn the "see more" click — story or bold insight.
- **Format:** short paragraphs, line breaks, a narrative arc, takeaway at the end.
- **CTA:** ask a question to drive comments. **Media:** native doc/image helps.
- **Best times:** Tue-Thu 8-10am local. **Cadence:** 3-5/week.

## Instagram
- **Ideal caption:** 125-600 chars (cap 2200). **Hashtags:** 5-15 (in caption or first comment).
- **Hook:** first line before the "more" cut. **Media:** required — carousel/Reel.
- **Format:** emoji-friendly, line breaks, aspirational tone.
- **CTA:** save / share / tag a friend. **Best times:** 11am-1pm & 7-9pm local.
- **Cadence:** 3-7 posts/week + daily stories.

## TikTok
- **Caption:** 50-150 chars. **Hashtags:** 3-6 (mix broad + niche + trend).
- **Hook:** the on-screen/spoken hook in the first 3 seconds is everything.
- **Media:** vertical video, native feel, trend/sound-aware.
- **CTA:** "follow for part 2", comment bait. **Best times:** Tue-Thu 6-10am & 7-11pm.
- **Cadence:** 1-4/day.

## YouTube Shorts
- **Title/desc:** 40-120 chars. **Hashtags:** 2-4 incl. #shorts.
- **Hook:** curiosity-gap title + first-3-seconds retention hook.
- **Media:** vertical video; strong thumbnail/first frame.
- **CTA:** subscribe. **Best times:** weekdays 12-3pm & 7-10pm. **Cadence:** 3-7/week.

## Threads
- **Ideal length:** 80-400 chars (cap 500). **Hashtags:** 0-1.
- **Hook:** casual, conversational, reply-baiting. **Media:** optional image.
- **CTA:** ask, invite replies. **Best times:** 9am-12pm & 6-8pm. **Cadence:** 2-6/day.

## Facebook
- **Ideal length:** 100-500 chars. **Hashtags:** 0-2.
- **Hook:** relatable, story-led. **Media:** image/video strongly favored.
- **CTA:** share / comment; great for Groups. **Best times:** weekdays 9am-1pm.
- **Cadence:** 3-7/week.

## Reddit
- **Ideal length:** 300-2500 chars. **Hashtags:** NONE (they're penalized).
- **Hook:** authentic title that fits the subreddit; no marketing voice.
- **Format:** value-first, well-structured, non-promotional. **Media:** optional.
- **CTA:** none/soft — Redditors reject overt CTAs. **Best times:** weekdays 6-9am EST.
- **Cadence:** match the target subreddit; quality over volume.

## Pinterest
- **Description:** 100-500 chars. **Hashtags:** 2-5, keyword-rich.
- **Hook:** searchable, keyword-front-loaded, evergreen how-to/inspiration.
- **Media:** tall (2:3) image/graphic required. **CTA:** "save for later".
- **Best times:** evenings 8-11pm & weekends. **Cadence:** 5-15 pins/week.

---

### Adaptation checklist (apply per target)
1. Rewrite the **hook** to the platform's style.
2. Resize to the **ideal length** band.
3. Set **hashtag count** to the platform's range (none for Reddit).
4. Match **tone** and add line breaks for **format**.
5. Add a fitting **CTA** (or omit on Reddit).
6. Note **posting time** + **cadence**.
7. Run `compose_check.py` to confirm the post passes the platform's rules.
