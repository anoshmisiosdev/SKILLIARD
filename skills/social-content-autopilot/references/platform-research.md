# Platform research basis

Reviewed 2026-06-20. Prefer current first-party guidance and account-specific
analytics over universal hacks. Ranking is personalized and changes; these
sources support principles, not guaranteed reach.

## Instagram

- Meta's [Instagram Best Practices announcement](https://about.fb.com/news/2024/10/best-practices-education-hub-creators-instagram/)
  says the Professional Dashboard provides current, personalized guidance across
  creation, engagement, reach, monetization, and guidelines. It explicitly
  covers attention, Reel length, posting frequency, metrics, algorithms, and the
  relationship between Reels and follower growth. Check this in-app guidance
  when account access exists instead of asserting a universal cadence or time.
- Meta's [original-content recommendation update](https://about.fb.com/ltam/news/2024/05/ayudando-a-los-creadores-a-encontrar-nuevas-audiencias/)
  says Instagram substitutes original content for identical reposts in
  recommendations and can make repeat aggregators ineligible. This supports the
  original/meaningfully transformed media rule.
- Meta's [Recommendation Guidelines overview](https://about.fb.com/news/2020/08/recommendation-guidelines/)
  distinguishes content allowed on the service from content eligible for broad
  recommendation. Check Account Status before treating reach as only a creative
  problem.

Operational inference: build discovery creative for attention and continued
viewing, and build useful/relatable content people willingly send or save. Do
not claim a fixed signal weight; validate with current Instagram Insights.

## LinkedIn

- LinkedIn's [feed-ranking explanation](https://www.linkedin.com/help/linkedin/answer/a9554004)
  says the feed considers hundreds of signals from post context, profile,
  network, and activity. This does not support one universal “algorithm hack.”
- LinkedIn's [content recommendation guidance](https://www.linkedin.com/blog/content-recommendations-avoid)
  favors helpful professional insights and meaningful conversations. It warns
  that pure promotion, engagement bait, engagement pods, irrelevant hashtags or
  tags, and unoriginal posts may not receive broader recommendation.
- LinkedIn's [post analytics documentation](https://www.linkedin.com/help/linkedin/answer/a516971/analytics-for-your-posts-articles-and-videos?lang=en)
  exposes in-network/out-of-network impressions, reach, profile activity,
  follows, reactions, comments, reposts, saves, sends, link visits, and video
  watch time. Select metrics by objective rather than treating likes as success.
- A sample of [r/LinkedInLunatics](https://www.reddit.com/r/LinkedInLunatics/)
  posts and discussions was reviewed as a satirical style corpus. Recurrent
  mechanics include dramatic hooks, broetry whitespace, inflated parables,
  business-lesson pivots, humblebrags, and “Agree?” closes. Treat these as
  creative observations, not evidence that the tactics improve distribution.

## X

- X's [organic best practices](https://business.x.com/en/basics/organic-best-practices)
  recommend concise conversational copy, avoiding hashtags, using a clear CTA
  when applicable, useful media, short captioned video, community response, and
  repeated testing. Treat its video-duration advice as a starting point for
  brand creative, not an organic ranking guarantee.
- X's [recommendation overview](https://help.x.com/en/rules-and-policies/recommendations)
  says recommendations use many signals and no signal has one static universal
  weight. This supports account-specific testing rather than a rigid recipe.
- [The X Rules](https://help.x.com/en/rules-and-policies/x-rules) prohibit
  platform manipulation and spam. Do not automate artificial amplification,
  aggressive engagement, or deceptive behavior.

## Evidence policy

Separate three levels in strategy notes:

1. **Hard constraint:** current platform specification or policy.
2. **First-party recommendation:** platform guidance, not a guarantee.
3. **Hypothesis:** a creative or timing choice to test against the account's
   recent comparable-post median.

Never present third-party benchmark times, hashtag counts, or correlation
studies as ranking facts. If current information is necessary, re-check the
linked official pages and the platform's in-product analytics before advising.
