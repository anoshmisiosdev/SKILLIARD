# Instagram

Build the visual first; the caption cannot rescue weak creative. Choose the
format from the job:

- **Reel:** discovery, demonstration, transformation, personality, or story.
- **Carousel:** a useful framework, comparison, checklist, or sequence worth
  saving and sending.
- **Single image:** one visually strong proof point or announcement.
- **Stories:** existing-audience interaction, follow-up, and conversion; not the
  primary discovery asset.

## Draft rules

1. Use original or meaningfully transformed media. Do not depend on a repost,
   visible third-party watermark, or copied template.
2. Make the first frame understandable without audio. State a concrete promise,
   tension, result, or demonstration immediately.
3. For a Reel, write a shot-aware script with fast evidence and no throat
   clearing. Add accurate captions. End as soon as the payoff is complete.
4. For a carousel, make slide 1 promise the outcome; make each slide advance one
   step; make the final slide summarize or give the next action.
5. Write a caption that adds context, proof, or a useful takeaway instead of
   transcribing the asset. Front-load the important sentence.
6. Create a natural reason to send or save: utility, identity, surprise,
   emotional resonance, or a concise reference. Do not ask for a save when the
   content is not worth revisiting.
7. Use plain-language topic keywords in the spoken words, on-screen text, and
   caption. Add only a few genuinely relevant hashtags, or none. Never add a
   generic hashtag block.
8. Use one CTA matched to the objective. Put a destination link in the profile
   or appropriate sticker when the surface does not make caption links useful.
9. Supply alt text or a useful visual description; keep essential text inside
   safe areas and readable on a phone.

## Output additions

Include the chosen surface, aspect ratio, opening frame, on-screen hook, shot or
slide sequence, caption, cover concept, and audio/caption note. If the user has
no asset, provide a production-ready creative brief rather than pretending the
caption alone is publishable.

Validate the caption with:
`python scripts/check.py --platform instagram --text "<caption>" --media`
