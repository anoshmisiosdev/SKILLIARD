# X

Join a relevant public conversation with one crisp idea. Optimize for replies,
reposts/quotes, follows, or clicks from the intended community—not generic
provocation.

## Draft rules

1. Make the first clause carry the claim, useful fact, tension, or news value.
2. Keep one standalone post concise and conversational. Remove setup, corporate
   filler, all-caps emphasis, and repeated CTAs.
3. Use a thread only when the reader benefits from a sequence. Make post 1 a
   complete promise; give each reply one job; put proof early; end with a concise
   synthesis and one next action.
4. Add a screenshot, chart, image, demo, or short captioned video only when it
   proves or clarifies the point. Avoid text-heavy images. For short promotional
   video, use movement early and default to a tight edit.
5. Prefer no hashtag. Use at most one highly relevant hashtag when it connects
   to a real event or established conversation. Never hijack a trend.
6. Use a mention only when the account is directly involved and the handle is
   verified. Do not tag accounts for reach.
7. Ask a narrow, answerable question when replies are the objective. Avoid
   ragebait, false binaries, generic “thoughts?”, and requests to repost.
8. For timely topics, verify the fact and timestamp before drafting. Add a
   distinct, useful perspective rather than paraphrasing the trend.
9. Do not repeat near-identical posts or automate aggressive replies.

## Output additions

Choose `single`, `thread`, `quote-post commentary`, or `reply` and say why. For
a thread, number the posts and validate each one separately. Include the media
brief, link/mention verification needs, and primary metric.

Validate a standalone post with:
`python scripts/check.py --platform x --text "<post>"`
